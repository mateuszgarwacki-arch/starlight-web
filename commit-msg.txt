S51: chunk cut plan into per-page units with proper chrome

Mateusz: "Still no good - it is not breaking it into the pages - it
shows page 4 for 3 pages - so at the bottom a printout, it shows 13
pages, but ready to print 16... Each page should have a header, a
bottom bit etc. - it all should be uniform"

The previous flowLayout single-page approach let the cut plan spill
across multiple printed pages via page-break-inside: auto, but the
Page chrome (job header / scope / step indicator / page footer) only
renders once on the first printed page and once on the last. Middle
printed pages were bare, and the page counter said "4 of 13" on the
final cut-plan page while the actual printed total was 16.

Fix: chunk the cut plan at the JS layer and render one <Page> per
chunk.

- src/lib/cut-layout.ts
  - New CutPlanPageChunk type and buildCutPlanPages() helper.
  - Sheet materials chunked into pages of SHEETS_PER_PAGE=10 (2-up
    grid). Timber materials chunked into BINS_PER_PAGE=25 (1-up).
  - First chunk overall carries isFirst=true so the renderer shows
    the "Suggested cut plan / Verify each sheet..." preamble.
  - Anomalies carried on the first chunk of each material only.

- src/components/cut-layout-renderer.tsx
  - New CutPlanPage component renders one chunk: preamble (first
    only), material header, anomalies, SVG slice.
  - CutPlanSection and MaterialCutPlan unchanged - still used by
    the live preview in the BOM extractor (no print, no chunking
    needed).

- src/app/traveller/page.tsx
  - WOData gets cutPlanChunks: CutPlanPageChunk[], computed once
    in loadData alongside cutPlanSummaries.
  - totalPages count loop: replace hasCutPlanPage boolean with
    totalPages += data.cutPlanChunks.length.
  - Render loop: replace the single flowLayout Page + CutPlanSection
    with a for-loop emitting one regular <Page> (no flowLayout) per
    chunk + <CutPlanPage chunk={chunk} />.

- docs/TRACKER.md
  - S51 "Initial placement and refinement" section: added the
    flowLayout-chrome bug + chunking fix paragraphs.

No schema. No DB writes. No new files at the storage layer.
