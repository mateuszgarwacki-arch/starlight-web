S38 timesheet gap detection (Stage 1)

Problem: freelancers log partial hours (5h on a 10h day) and Mateusz
has to chase. Now automated.

Data:
- tbl_timesheet_flags (freelancer_id, flag_date, expected/logged hours,
  status, reason). UNIQUE(freelancer_id, flag_date). RLS: freelancers
  see/update their own; admin/pm see all.
- Registered in AUDITED_TABLES as flag_id.

Detection:
- rpc_detect_timesheet_gaps(flag_date) — for the given date, finds
  freelancers who logged some hours but under 90% of their standard day
  (default 10h). Upserts to tbl_timesheet_flags. Idempotent:
  re-running refreshes logged_hours on open flags; auto-closes flags
  if hours now hit 90%; leaves resolved flags alone.
- pg_cron schedule 'timesheet-gap-detect-daily' at 0 6 * * * UTC runs
  the detector for CURRENT_DATE - 1 day.

UI:
- components/timesheet-flags.tsx: TimesheetFlagsBanner (red strip with
  count, tap to /m/me), TimesheetFlagsPanel (date rows with "Add hours"
  deep-link and "Give reason" chip picker + note).
- /m page: banner at top.
- /m/me page: full panel above stats.
- /m/task: reads ?date= and ?short= URL params, pre-fills LogSheet date
  and hours, shows red banner explaining context.

Admin:
- /review/timesheets: open flags table (freelancer, date, logged/expected,
  short-hours, raised date, admin-close button) + collapsible resolved
  section showing how each was closed (logged / reason / admin) with the
  reason note.

Verified against live data:
- Cron SQL returns 2 flags for 2026-04-22 (Karol 7.5h, Kamran 3.25h);
  Mark and Martin clean at 10h.
- Re-running the detector is a no-op.
